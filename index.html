    2. Projects for IB

    Throughout the IB (International Baccalaureate) program, IB candidates are required to complete IAs (Internal Assessments) which are papers/projects for each specific class, and an EE (Extended Essay) which is a research paper on a topic of the candidate's choosing. Recorded below are a few of my favorite IAs that were completed throughout my time in the IB program.

    2.1 EE: Opioid Epidemic Research Paper

    For my EE topic I chose to do a research paper on the Opioid Epidemic through the means of a case study between California and West Virginia. Both of these states have faced the Opioide Epidemic, but to much different extents. Consequently, this paper delves into the causes for the varying severities of each epidemic such as: socio-economic, poltical, and educational factors. Furthermore, I also delve into the ways that each state has been managing their respective crises.


    The full research paper can be found here.


    2.2 Chemistry IA: Electrolysis Experimentation

    For my chemistry IA, I did an experiment involving the electrolysis of water using varying concentrations of the catalyst KOH (potassium hydroxide) to figure out how this effects the volume of HHO produced. The device used for the electrolysis is the HHO generator (further explained below). Unfortunately the data collection was limited because the power supply I had access to self-regulated itself and would shutoff in the middle of trials therefore data had to be extrapulated using long trials averaged where outliers were removed and an average was taken (more information is contained in the full report).

    3. Project S.P.A.R.K. Experiments/Projects

    When I was in 6th grade I was fortunate enough to be a part of a project-based STEM class. As time has gone on however, the resources available to children in Flagler County (my home county) have decreased dramatially. As such, I co-founded a STEM enrichment club, Project S.P.A.R.K., which has currently been implemented in three of the Title I elementary schools in Flagler County. The primary goal of project S.P.A.R.K. has been to 'spark' the imaginations, creativity, critical-thinking, and problem solving skills of the children involved. Since experiences as a kid can have a huge impact on their future aspirations, it is of the utmost importance to stimulate these young minds. Who knows, maybe one day the children touched by S.P.A.R.K. will one day become scientists or engineers making world changing advancements.

    3.1 School Launch Days

    Located above are some pictures from the highly successful launch days.


    As myself and the other co-founders planned ways to implement what we had thought of in the 2022-2023 school year, we decided that it would be best to go to one school towards the end of the year and work with their students on mini-projects to generate interest and test the viability. Myself and nine of my classmates went to Bunnell Elementary school and ran a project with their 2nd, 3rd, and 4th graders following the standards they had learned throughout the school year. With the 2nd graders we made rain clouds in jars, with the 3rd graders we created oobleck, and last but not least, with the 4th graders we made spaghetti and marshmallow towers. Although relatively simple in nature, the students showed immense interest and seemed to succesfully draw connections to what they had been learning in the classroom. Based on these reactions alone, we decided that this project was more than worth our time. We are inspiring our world's future.

    As I have grown older, creation has become an important aspect of my identity. I always find myself trying to get my hands messy with whatever project idea I come up with. While I haven't had the most time, I still try to feed my internal DIYer urges through whatever means possible.


    4.1 HHO Generator

    When I was a kid I had seen a video on the creation of an HHO generator. Although I didn't know it then, I would soon learn that this process of splitting water into its constituent componets was called electrolysis. Ever since that day, I have been fascinated with this subject and the multitude of uses that arise from the hydrogen and oxygen gas that is produced. When I finally had the resources and know-how, I built my own version of an HHO generator (still heavily based upon the presentation of The King of Random's). This is the starter to a series of projects that I intend to build in the future which will run on the gasses produced by the generator. For example, I intend to create a mini hydroxy torch which will be run using the gasses from the HHO generator itself.
