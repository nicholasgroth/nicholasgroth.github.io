1. Projects for IB

Throughout the IB program, we as candidates, are required to do a project, or Internal Assessment (IA), for each class. On top of this, the IB program requires an extended 4000 word research paper, or EE.

1.1 Opioid Epidemic Research Paper

When researching for the EE, I had always been intrigued by the war on drugs and the socio-economic, political, and criminal justice reprecussions as a result of the war. In more recent years, the opioid epidemic has become a major public health crisis in the United States, and the issues that addicts have suffered from have really resonated with me as a person. The research question I chose for this essay is:What are the key differences and similarities between the opioid crises in California and West Virginia, and how have these factors influenced the approach to managing the crisis in each state? The goal of this research paper is to analyze the effectiveness of different treatment methods for opioid addiction, and to analyze how differences in resources can affect a state's ability to manage this public health crisis. 2.1 HHO Generator

I have always been fascinated by the process of water electrolysis. This is the process of separating water into its component parts, hydrogen and oxygen, through the use of electricity conducted through the use of an electrolyte (in this case potassium hydroxide or KOH). There are numerous potential uses for the gasses produced, specifically hydrogen, including their use as supplemental fuel to internal combustion engines. This specific design features a combined output, meaning that the gasses come out of one output together rather than separate. As a result, the gasses are highly reactive and I intend to safely experiment on building bottle rockets using this gas. In the future, I plan on creating a separate output HHO generator as well for separate future experiments using the two gasses separately rather than combined.


2.2 STEM Enrichment and Outreach Program

Throughout my years of schooling in Flagler County, I have noticed a major problem in the education system: the lack of STEM oppurtunities for students. Fortunately, I was lucky and was able to participate in a STEM heavy class in 6th grade; however, past this enrichment, I have had no further STEM enrichment. To try to give to my community what I was not able to receive. With the help of some of my classmates I have created a STEM outreach program for students in Flagler County.

To kick start the program, at the end of the 2022-2023 school year, we went out to one of the larger title I schools in Flagler County, Bunnell Elementary School. We ran STEM experiments/demonstrations with the upcoming 3-5th graders (current 2-4th graders) to start generating a greater interest in STEM in Flagler County. With the 4th graders we built spaghetti and marshmallow towers to demonstrate and tied in their standards of mass, weight, and gravity, with the 3rd graders we created oobleck to tie in their states of matter standard, and finally with the 2nd graders we created rain clouds in a bottle to tie in their standard of weather.

For the start of the following school year (2023-2024) we plan on establishing a STEM enrichment club for the large title I elementary schools in the county, Bunnell Elementary, Wadsworth Elementary, and Rymfire Elementary. Further into the year, we hope to expand this club into all fascets of Flagler County to provide all students with the STEM enrichment oppurtunities that encourage students to pursue carreers in STEM, or rather just have fun to foster creativity, critical thinking, and applied learning of topics in their school standards. Nonetheless, this project's mission is to provide all students of Flagler County with the opportunities that many of my classmates and I were not offerred throughout our schooling in Flagler County.
